# Mood Board Studio by [Faini Made](https://fainimade.com)

A fast, private, browser-based tool for assembling image mood boards and exporting them as print-ready PDFs. No account, no uploads, no cloud. Everything runs locally on your machine.

---

## What it's for

This is not a replacement for contact sheets in Capture One, Lightroom, or Bridge. It's for those moments when someone wants to quickly see the continuity, vibe, or a sample of what was shot in a day, without the overhead of a full DAM workflow. Toss some images in, generate a board, export a PDF.

---

## Quick start

1. **Drop or browse** your images into the panel on the left
2. Set your **page size**, **orientation**, and any **title / subtitle**
3. Click **Generate Board**
4. If you don't love the layout, click **Generate Board** again. The engine re-shuffles and re-packs until you find one you like
5. Click **Export PDF** when you're happy

---

## Tips

**Start with your title.** Enter it before you generate. The title and subtitle are baked into the bar at the top (or bottom) of each page. Font, size, and alignment changes update instantly without regenerating the whole board.

**Margins and gaps.** The defaults work well for images with consistent aspect ratios. If you're mixing portrait and landscape shots heavily, bumping the margin up a notch can help the layout breathe.

**Deleting images from the preview.** Click any image on a board page to select it. A red X button appears in the centre. Click the X to remove that image. The remaining images reflow across all pages automatically.

**Adding images mid-session.** You can add more images at any time using the drop zone. Hit Generate Board again to incorporate them.

**Image order.** By default images are sorted randomly. This helps the layout engine distribute aspect ratios more evenly across rows. Switch to **Filename** order in the sidebar if you need a specific sequence.

**Columns and rows.** Leave both on **Auto** for the organic, proportional layout. Set fixed columns or rows when you want a more structured grid.

**Avoid single-image pages.** On by default in Auto mode. The layout engine will push a lone image to the next page so it shares space with at least one other. Turn it off if you want full-page hero images.

## Supported formats

| Format | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| JPEG / JPG | Full support |
| PNG | Full support |
| WebP | Full support in all modern browsers |
| HEIC / HEIF | Natively supported in Safari on macOS and iOS. Use JPEG or WebP for Chrome and Firefox |

---

## Export

Boards export as **PDF** at full resolution, sized to:

- **US Letter** - 8.5 x 11 in
- **A4** - 210 x 297 mm

Both portrait and landscape orientations are supported.

---

## Privacy

Everything runs in your browser. Images are never uploaded to any server. There is no server. Close the tab and nothing persists. The tool works fully offline once the page has loaded.

---

## Known limitations

- HEIC files require Safari and will not decode correctly in Chrome or Firefox
- Very large batches (50+ high-resolution images) may be slow to import on lower-end hardware
- Layout is randomised. There is no manual drag-to-reorder within a page
